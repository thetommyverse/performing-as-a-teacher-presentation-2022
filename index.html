PowerPoint Etiquette
is hugely important

And it is not a problem exclusive to PowerPoint.
This applies to any form of presentational media

People underestimate how good a
well-prepared PowerPoint presentation is

Hence, I have prepared a few examples of
important aspects to make your presentations...
engaging,
interesting,
and not overwhelming

b) Don't Overwhelm your Slides

(like I did on the previous slide)
Your audience will find it messy and struggle to pull out the key terms

  • Put the 'simplest' version of your points on the slide
    • 7% increase in sales this month
      • vs.
    • There has been a 7 percent increase in our companies sales this month

  • Talk around your points
    • Today's Topic: Binary Numbers

  • Don't be afraid of Bullet Points
    • They can be Notes
    • or Reminders

    Do not write everything you are going to say on your slides
    • That is what Speaker Notes are for. A handy feature available in all your popular presentation programs
Many people take notes in bullet points anyways. Bullet points can clearly organize the information and help your audience follow it.

c) Animations are great!
But Dangerous!

  • Animations can really draw attention to your text
  • They need to be quick and subtle
  • If they are 'too slow' they can draw the audience's attention away
  • Don't use pointless animations!

    • Keep them simple! Relevant! Minimal!

    • With no pointless spins
    • Or randomly changing colours

...c) and Transitions as well!


  • Fancy Transitions are fun!
    • But should not be overused

  • Stick to the simple, but professional slide transitions

Idea No. 3.

Proper Presentation Etiquette

  • Clear Layout and Design Language
  • Don't overwhelm your slides with information
  • Don't overuse animations and slide transitions
